Butterfly Tattoos -

Monica Huynh

I stamp butterflies on my body like all the red lights I have run,

all the heartless compliments, all the unfinished kisses. A lover from

a distant time asked me what all of this meant and I had no answer.

Life lately is The Strokes’ Ode to the Mets and I am that Drums Please Fab line

and they kept asking why. I want to be honest and pure like a stranger’s praise,

a broken-hearted poem, a loved one coming home to tell me they have

my favorite cake — but I am all that I am not, a bustling crowd, a handshake,

a message that never got replied, a wet dream, an Instagram story, a technical

documentation of all the things that are supposed to feel futuristic. So I stamp

butterflies on my body like I wish for belonging. My vocabulary is now corporate offices

and gen Z self-care, but my heart lives in that smokey jazz bar where a kiss from my

lover tastes like summer at its zenith. I can always be someone’s con or em or mày and

that’s life’s greatest blessing - I went so far from my hometown just to come running back

to sun-kissed wrinkles, folklores and neglected poets. Spring left me a poem in

my lover’s eyes when he told me all about Odyssey and Penelope. Baby don’t you know

home is within us. What loves us, always returns. But sometimes all I can see are vast seas,

lost men and sounds of butterfly wings cascading to goodbyes at skytrain stations and hugs

we didn’t know to be the last. So here I turn to butterfly tattoos, trembling voices and

scarring touches — I long for transformation so I win the war. I fly home with my wings,

full of wounds, and

full of love.
